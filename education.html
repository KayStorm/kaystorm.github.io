I am a first generation college student. I considered learning to be my exit strategy from an unhappy home life. I wanted to "make it out."

Once I was out, I decided I wanted to help other kids find their ways out, so I became a teacher. I graduated from Appalachian State University with a degree in Middle Grades Education. My concentrations were in Social Studies and English Language Arts.

After 6 years teaching ELA, I decided I wanted to make myself into a History specialist and enrolled in grad school at UNCW. I was hopeful this would give me deeper knowledge to share with students, and potentially be a path to working with older students. I focused my studies on modern American History. I completed my thesis research on President Nixon's somewhat secret attempts to save the space program, even though many blame him for the end of the Apollo program.

I was teaching at the local Adult High School when Covid jarred the world. I watched all my teacher friends struggle to transition to a digital-dependent world. I decided that I was ready to leave the classroom and move into the EdTech field, so I picked up a few classes at the community college. It let me test my own skills and interest, and I fell in love with the field.

I knew I needed more advanced learning than what CFCC could offer, so I am now enrolled in classes at East Carolina University. In roughly 1.5 years I will graduate with a Masters Certificate in Computer Based Instruction.
